## STUDY DESIGN

Response = outcome measured  
Factor = explanatory variable  
Treatment = level/combo of factors  
Experimental Unit = smallest unit receiving treatment  
Covariate = measured but not manipulated  

Randomization → removes systematic bias  
Replication → reduces variance  
Control → fair comparison  

Observational → assignment mechanism unknown  
Experimental → assignment mechanism controlled  

---

## RANDOM VARIABLES

$X$ random variable  
$x$ observed value  
$\bar X = \frac{1}{n} \sum X_i$  
$\bar x$ observed mean  

### PMF
$P(X=x)$, $\sum p(x)=1$

### PDF
$f(x)\ge0$, $\int f(x)dx=1$

### CDF
$F(x)=P(X\le x)$

---

## DISTRIBUTIONS

### Bernoulli(p)
$E=p$  
$Var=p(1-p)$

### Binomial(n,p)
$P(X=k)=\binom{n}{k}p^k(1-p)^{n-k}$  
$E=np$  
$Var=np(1-p)$

### Normal($\mu,\sigma^2$)
$E=\mu$, $Var=\sigma^2$  

Standardize:
$Z=\frac{X-\mu}{\sigma}$

---

## EXPECTATION & VARIANCE

$E(aX+b)=aE(X)+b$  
$Var(aX+b)=a^2Var(X)$

$Var(X+Y)=Var(X)+Var(Y)+2Cov(X,Y)$  
$Var(X-Y)=Var(X)+Var(Y)-2Cov(X,Y)$

$Cov(aX,bY)=abCov(X,Y)$

$\rho=\frac{Cov(X,Y)}{\sigma_X\sigma_Y}$

If independent → $Cov=0$

---

## SAMPLE MEAN

$\bar X=\frac{1}{n}\sum X_i$

$E(\bar X)=\mu$  
$Var(\bar X)=\frac{\sigma^2}{n}$  
$SD(\bar X)=\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n}$

$Var(a\bar X+b)=a^2\frac{\sigma^2}{n}$

Sample variance:
$S^2=\frac{1}{n-1}\sum (X_i-\bar X)^2$

If normal & $\sigma$ unknown:
$T=\frac{\bar X-\mu}{S/\sqrt n} \sim t_{n-1}$

---

## HYPOTHESIS TESTING

$H_0: \theta=\theta_0$  
$H_A: \neq, >, <$

Mean (σ known):
$z=\frac{\bar x-\mu_0}{\sigma/\sqrt n}$

Reject (two-sided):
$|z|>z_{\alpha/2}$

Type I = $\alpha$  
Type II = $\beta$  
Power = $1-\beta$  

Empirical Rule:  
1 SD → 68%  
2 SD → 95%  
3 SD → 99.7%  

---

## PROPORTIONS

$\hat p=\frac{X}{n}$

$E(\hat p)=p$  
$Var(\hat p)=\frac{p(1-p)}{n}$

Test:
$z=\frac{\hat p-p_0}{\sqrt{p_0(1-p_0)/n}}$

CI:
$\hat p \pm z_{\alpha/2}\sqrt{\frac{\hat p(1-\hat p)}{n}}$

Sample size:
$n=\frac{z^2 p(1-p)}{m^2}$

Worst case → $p=0.5$

---

## TWO TREATMENTS

Unpaired:
$Var=\frac{\sigma_A^2}{n_A}+\frac{\sigma_B^2}{n_B}$

Balanced:
$\frac{\sigma_A^2+\sigma_B^2}{n}$

Equal variances:
$\frac{2\sigma^2}{n}$

Paired:
$Var(\bar D)=\frac{\sigma_A^2+\sigma_B^2-2Cov(X,Y)}{n}$

Paired better if $Cov>0$

---

## CRD

$\binom{N}{N_A}=\frac{N!}{N_A!(N-N_A)!}$

Each assignment:
$1/\binom{N}{N_A}$

Propensity:
$P(W_i=1)=\frac{N_A}{N}$

Units $i$ and $j$ both treated:
$\frac{\binom{N-2}{N_A-2}}{\binom{N}{N_A}}$

---

## POWER (One-Sided Mean)

Reject if:
$\bar X>\mu_0+z_\alpha\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n}$

Under $\mu_1$:
$Z\sim N\left(\frac{\mu_1-\mu_0}{\sigma/\sqrt n},1\right)$

Power:
$P\left(Z>z_\alpha-\frac{\mu_1-\mu_0}{\sigma/\sqrt n}\right)$

---

## INTERPRETATION

Confidence:
“If repeated many times, ___% of intervals contain the true parameter.”

p-value:
“If $H_0$ were true, this extreme would occur with probability ___.”

Reject if $p<\alpha$.
